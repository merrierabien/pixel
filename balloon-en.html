Ballon rétro




Pixel art is an exercise deeply connected with video games. On less performant machines, like the 8-bits for example, the display of the actions could be a real challenge for developers because of the low resolution of the screens and the limited storage capacity to store data. The Game Boy is a 8-bits video game console, which is a good example of these challenges. It only has 4 hues of gray to show everything on screen, plus the limitations like the maximum amount of sprites you can display at the same time and the screen dimensions of 160x144 pixels. The Game Boy impose its limitations, and that is a part of the common history between pixel art and video game.
Here, it is my first time trying to use a limited color palette. I tried to recreate what a Game Boy could display with its limitations, even if the dimensions are not the same.
